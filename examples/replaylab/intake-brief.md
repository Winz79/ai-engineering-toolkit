# ReplayLab Intake Brief

This is a worked example of the intake brief format. It intentionally stays at the intake level and does not invent a full product specification.

## 1. Request

Create an initial planning brief for ReplayLab using the AI-first engineering methodology.

## 2. Desired Outcome

Establish enough shared context to decide whether ReplayLab should proceed through full mode and what discovery questions must be answered next.

## 3. Users / Stakeholders

Known stakeholders are not defined in the source context.

Assumption: ReplayLab may involve people who need to review, replay, analyze, or learn from recorded workflows, sessions, events, or interactions. This assumption must be validated before requirements are written.

## 4. Current Situation

ReplayLab is named as an example project, but no confirmed business context, user group, product scope, technical stack, data model, or deployment target has been provided.

## 5. Scope

### In Scope

- Capture the initial request as an intake artifact.
- Identify missing context.
- Prepare for context discovery.
- Avoid defining product behavior before validation.

### Out of Scope

- Full BRD or PRD.
- Architecture proposal.
- Data model.
- Implementation plan.
- Technology selection.
- Security design.

## 6. Constraints

- Do not invent project-specific business context.
- Keep the example distinct from the generic methodology.
- Treat unknowns as open questions.
- Use full mode if ReplayLab becomes a new product or major system initiative.

## 7. Known Context

- ReplayLab is the example name for this repository's first worked example.
- The methodology requires intent, context, requirements, impact, design, delivery planning, and build/review/learn stages.
- No confirmed ReplayLab-specific domain context is available yet.

## 8. Open Questions

- What problem should ReplayLab solve?
- Who are the primary users?
- What does "replay" mean in this domain?
- What inputs does ReplayLab consume?
- What outputs or decisions should ReplayLab support?
- Are there security, privacy, or compliance concerns around replayed data?
- Is this a new product, an internal tool, or an enhancement to an existing system?
- What would a successful first vertical slice demonstrate?

## 9. Initial Risk Notes

- Replay or recording workflows can involve sensitive data.
- Product scope is ambiguous.
- The domain model is unknown.
- The word "lab" may imply experimentation, but that is not confirmed.
- Requirements should not be generated until context discovery validates the intended use case.

## 10. Suggested Mode

Full mode.

Reason: ReplayLab appears to be a new project example with unclear product, domain, data, and security context.
